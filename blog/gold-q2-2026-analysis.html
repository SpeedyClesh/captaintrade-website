Gold in Q2 2026 — Why I'm Still Long and Where I'm Looking to Add

XAU/USD has been one of the cleanest trending assets of the year. Here's my current read on the weekly structure, where the key levels are, and how I'm sizing my positions.

Weekly structure read

Gold has been in a clear higher-timeframe uptrend since the Q4 2024 breakout above the $2,100 level. Every significant pullback since then has found buyers, and the structure of higher highs and higher lows on the weekly chart remains intact as of this writing.

The most recent weekly candle formed a bullish engulfing pattern off the $2,280 support level — a level that has been tested and held three times in the past six months. That kind of multi-test respect is significant. It tells you there are buyers defending that level with size.

Current bias

Bullish on XAU/USD with a weekly target of $2,450–2,480. Holding long from $2,295 with a stop below $2,255. Risk is approximately 1.5% of account.

Macro context

Gold's strength in 2025-2026 has been driven by a combination of factors that haven't reversed: central bank accumulation (particularly from BRICS nations), persistent geopolitical uncertainty, and a dollar that has weakened relative to its 2024 highs.

The Fed's pause on rate hikes has removed one of the primary headwinds for gold. Real yields — the key driver of gold price in a macro context — have declined from their 2023 peaks, which creates a more favourable environment for non-yielding assets like gold.

I don't trade purely on macro fundamentals, but I use them as a directional filter. When macro and technicals align — as they do right now for gold — I'm willing to hold positions for longer and size up slightly.

How I'm sizing

My standard position sizing rule is 1% risk per trade. For this gold trade, given the strength of the setup and the macro alignment, I've sized at 1.5% — the maximum I'll go on any single position regardless of conviction.

The entry was at $2,295, stop at $2,255, giving me a 40-point risk. My first target at $2,420 gives a 1:3.1 risk-to-reward on the initial entry. If price reaches $2,420 and I close half, the remaining position runs to $2,480 with a stop moved to breakeven — so the second half is essentially free.

Invalidation

The trade is invalidated by a weekly close below $2,255. That would break the higher-low structure that has defined this trend and would signal that the institutional buyers who have been defending $2,280 have stepped away. In that scenario, I'd close the position immediately and reassess from scratch.

I'll post an update when price reaches either target or triggers the stop. Follow on X for real-time commentary.
